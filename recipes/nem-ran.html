Nem Ran - Thing must try in Vietnam

Description

Nem Rán, also known as Vietnamese fried spring rolls, is a beloved dish in Vietnamese cuisine, especially popular during family gatherings, holidays, and festivals. Each roll is filled with a mixture of minced pork, seafood, vegetables, glass noodles, and mushrooms, wrapped in rice paper and deep-fried to golden perfection.

Crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside, Nem Rán is typically served with fresh herbs, lettuce, and a dipping sauce called nước chấm. In southern Vietnam, this dish is known as chả giò, and it can be adapted with different fillings according to taste or seasonal ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 300 g minced pork
  • 100 g shrimp (peeled, deveined, finely chopped)
  • 50 g dried glass noodles (mung bean vermicelli), soaked and cut into short strands
  • 50 g wood ear mushrooms, soaked and finely chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, grated
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Rice paper wrappers
  • Cooking oil for frying

Steps

  1. Soak glass noodles and wood ear mushrooms in warm water until soft, then drain and chop.
  2. Combine minced pork, shrimp, noodles, mushrooms, carrot, onion, egg, fish sauce, and pepper in a large bowl. Mix well.
  3. Briefly dip each rice paper in warm water to soften. Place a spoonful of filling near the edge, fold in the sides, and roll tightly.
  4. Heat enough oil in a deep pan to submerge the rolls.
  5. Fry the rolls in batches over medium heat until golden brown and crispy, about 5–7 minutes.
  6. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  7. Serve hot with fresh herbs, lettuce, and nước chấm dipping sauce.
